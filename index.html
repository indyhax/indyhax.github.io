# Frequently Asked Questions

► What is a hackathon?
A hackathon is an event where people collaborate intensively on software projects over a short period of time. It's a mix of "hack" (meaning creative problem-solving, not breaking into systems) and "marathon." Participants form teams, brainstorm ideas, code solutions, and present their projects - all in a fun, fast-paced environment!
► Do I need to be an expert programmer?
Not at all! Hackathons welcome participants of all skill levels. Whether you're a beginner just learning to code or have some experience, you'll find ways to contribute. You can work on design, project ideas, presentations, or learn from your teammates. The goal is to learn and build something together!
► How do teams work at hackathons?
Teams typically have 2-5 members with diverse skills. You can form teams before the event or find teammates on the day of. Good teams include a mix of roles: coders, designers, idea generators, and presenters. Collaboration and communication are just as important as technical skills!
► What should I bring to a hackathon?
Bring your laptop (fully charged!), chargers, any necessary cables, and a positive attitude. Some hackathons provide food and drinks, but check ahead. Most importantly, bring your creativity and willingness to learn. Don't worry about having everything figured out in advance!
► What if I've never been to a hackathon before?
First-timers are always welcome! Hackathons are designed to be beginner-friendly learning experiences. Mentors and organizers are there to help you. You'll meet other students, learn new technologies, and gain experience working on real projects. Everyone starts somewhere, and hackathons are perfect for beginners!
► How long does a hackathon last?
Hackathons vary in length. Some are 24-hour events, others span a weekend, and some like IndyHAX might be a single day event. The timeline typically includes an opening ceremony, hacking time, project submissions, presentations, and judging. You'll have enough time to build something meaningful!
► What can I learn from participating?
Hackathons teach you practical skills like coding, using APIs, teamwork, time management, and presenting ideas. You'll also gain exposure to new technologies (like AI), meet fellow students interested in tech, build your portfolio, and discover what you're capable of creating in a short time. Plus, they're fun!
► Are there prizes?
Many hackathons offer prizes for winning teams, but the real prizes are the experience, skills, and connections you gain. Prizes can include tech gear, gift cards, certificates, or even opportunities for further development of your project. But remember: everyone who participates and learns something new is a winner!
